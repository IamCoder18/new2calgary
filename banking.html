💳 Banking in Calgary

Banks, accounts, and money tips for newcomers

Opening a Canadian bank account is one of the first things you should do. Most banks offer newcomer packages with no fees for the first 1-2 years. Here's what you need to know.

🏦 Major Banks in Canada

TD Canada Trust

One of the biggest banks with many branches. Good newcomer packages.

  • Newcomer account (no fees for 1-2 years)
  • Easy online banking
  • Good customer service in multiple languages
Tip: Visit a branch with your passport and study permit/work permit to open an account.

Royal Bank (RBC)

Canada's biggest bank. Great for newcomers and students.

  • Newcomer program with no fees
  • Free student account (for those in school)
  • Good credit building options

Scotiabank

Third-largest bank, popular among immigrants.

  • Newcomer packages available
  • International transfers made easier
  • Good mobile app

CIBC

Another big bank with newcomer-friendly options.

  • No-fee newcomer account
  • Easy to build credit history

📋 What You Need to Open an Account

  • Passport - Required for identification
  • Proof of status - Study permit, work permit, or PR card
  • Proof of address - Rental agreement or utility bill (can often use hotel address initially)
  • Social Insurance Number (SIN) - Not always required to open account, but needed for banking fully

🏧 No-Fee Digital Banks

Simplii Financial

Online bank by CIBC. No fees, no minimum balance.

  • Completely free accounts
  • Free ATM withdrawals at CIBC ATMs
  • Easy to sign up online

Tangerine

Online bank by Scotiabank. Good for those who want no fees.

  • No monthly fees
  • Free transfers
  • Good savings rates

💡 Tips for Newcomers

  • Start building credit early - Get a credit card and pay it off monthly
  • Use bank referral codes - Both you and the referrer can get bonuses
  • Compare packages - Each bank has different newcomer offers
  • Don't rush - Take your time to understand your options
